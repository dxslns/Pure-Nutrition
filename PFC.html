Understanding Macronutrients

Everything you need to know about Proteins, Fats, and Carbohydrates

What Are Macronutrients?

Macronutrients are the nutrients your body requires in large amounts for energy and bodily functions. The three main macronutrients are protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Each has a specific role in the body and provides a different amount of calories per gram.

Protein

Proteins are made of amino acids and are essential for building and repairing tissues, producing enzymes and hormones, and supporting immune function. Each gram of protein provides 4 kcal. Good sources include meat, fish, dairy, legumes, and tofu.

Carbohydrates

Carbs are your body's primary source of energy. They break down into glucose, which fuels your cells. Each gram provides 4 kcal. Focus on complex carbs like whole grains, fruits, and vegetables rather than refined sugars.

Fats

Fats support cell growth, hormone production, and nutrient absorption. Each gram of fat provides 9 kcal. Choose healthy fats such as those from avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil. Limit trans and saturated fats.

Macronutrient Balance

The right balance depends on your goals and lifestyle. A common ratio for general health is 40% carbs, 30% protein, and 30% fat. For weight loss or muscle gain, adjustments can be made accordingly.
