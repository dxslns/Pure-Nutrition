Understanding Essential Minerals

Discover the role of key minerals like magnesium and iron, how they function, and where to get them.

What Are Minerals?

Minerals are inorganic elements that your body needs to perform vital functions such as building bones, transmitting nerve impulses, and producing hormones. They are categorized into macrominerals (needed in larger amounts) and trace minerals (needed in smaller amounts).

Magnesium

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions, including muscle and nerve function, blood glucose control, and energy production. It's found in leafy greens, nuts, seeds, and whole grains.

Iron

Iron is crucial for oxygen transport in the blood via hemoglobin and supports energy metabolism. Heme iron (from meat) is more bioavailable than non-heme iron (from plants). Iron-rich foods include red meat, spinach, and legumes.

Other Important Minerals

- Calcium: Bone health, muscle function.
- Potassium: Fluid balance, nerve signals.
- Zinc: Immune support, wound healing.
- Selenium: Antioxidant defense and thyroid function.

Deficiencies & Excess

Both deficiencies and excesses in minerals can disrupt body functions. For instance, iron deficiency causes anemia, while too much iron can damage organs. Balanced intake through a varied diet is key.
