Pork

Meat

78/100

Caloric content

Varies by cut. Tenderloin: ~143 kcal, Shoulder: ~220 kcal, Bacon: 400+ kcal per 100g. Fatty cuts significantly increase calorie count.

7/10

Energy value

High in both protein and fat. Provides substantial energy, especially in fattier cuts. Lean pork supports muscle growth.

8/10

Amino acid profile

Excellent source of complete protein with all essential amino acids. Comparable to beef in quality.

8/10

Cholesterol level

Moderate to high — approx. 60–80 mg per 100g. Should be eaten in moderation by those with cholesterol concerns.

6/10

Fat composition

Contains both saturated and monounsaturated fats. The fat profile is less ideal than fish or lean meats. High-fat cuts can promote inflammation.

6/10

Processing method

Consumed fresh, roasted, or processed (ham, bacon, sausage). Processed pork often contains additives and excess sodium.

7/10

Allergens

Pork allergies are rare but exist. Religious and cultural dietary restrictions are also a factor.

5/10
