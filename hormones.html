Hormones and Your Body

Understanding the chemical messengers that regulate nearly every bodily function and process

What Are Hormones?

Hormones are chemical messengers produced by endocrine glands that travel through the bloodstream to tissues and organs. They control many major bodily functions, from simple needs like hunger to complex systems like reproduction, and even emotions and mood.

Major Endocrine Glands

The human body contains several important glands that produce hormones, including the pituitary (the "master gland"), thyroid, adrenal glands, pancreas, ovaries (in females), and testes (in males). Each gland produces specific hormones that regulate different bodily functions.

Key Hormones and Their Functions

Insulin regulates blood sugar, cortisol manages stress response, thyroid hormones control metabolism, estrogen and testosterone govern sexual development and reproduction, growth hormone affects development, and melatonin regulates sleep cycles.

Hormonal Balance

Hormonal balance is crucial for health. Too much or too little of a hormone can cause significant health issues. Factors like stress, diet, exercise, sleep, and environmental toxins can all affect hormonal balance in the body.

Hormones and Metabolism

Several hormones work together to regulate metabolism. Thyroid hormones set the metabolic rate, insulin and glucagon regulate blood sugar, leptin and ghrelin control hunger signals, and cortisol influences energy availability during stress.

Hormonal Changes Through Life

Hormone levels naturally change throughout life - during puberty, menstrual cycles, pregnancy, and menopause in women, and andropause in men. Understanding these changes helps manage expectations and health during different life stages.
